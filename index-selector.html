The problem

Spreadsheets lie about queues.

Queueing formulas assume steady-state averages. Real systems do not work that way, and the gap between the formula and reality grows fast as utilisation rises.

  • ⚡
    Variability is non-linear

    At high utilisation, waiting time rises far faster than intuitive spreadsheet estimates suggest.

  • 📊
    Distributions matter

    Point estimates hide variance. Simulation exposes ranges and confidence, not just averages.

  • 🔗
    Systems interact

    Blocking, shared resources, and routing effects create behaviour formulas cannot predict.

Worked example

A&E triage, modelled in 20 minutes.

Two-stage emergency pathway

Patients arrive at an average rate of 12 per hour. High-acuity patients are prioritised. Low-acuity patients share constrained clinician capacity.

A spreadsheet can estimate one queue. A simulation shows the trade-off between patient groups, the confidence around that estimate, and where the system is structurally failing.
